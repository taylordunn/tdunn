What is the relationship between the individual gradings and the overall total_cup_points? There are 10 gradings with scores 0-10, so I assume adding them together gives total_cup_points, which ranges 0-100:

coffee %>%
  rowwise() %>%
  transmute(
    total_cup_points,
    sum_gradings = sum(c_across(aroma:cupper_points))
  ) %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = total_cup_points, y = sum_gradings)) +
  geom_point(color = coffee_pal[1], size = 2) +
  geom_abline(size = 1)

Some very slight deviations, but yes my assumption is correct. A second assumption: there will be mostly positive associations among the individual gradings (e.g. a coffee with high flavor will have high body on average). Compute and plot the pairwise correlations with GGally:

coffee %>%
  select(aroma:cupper_points) %>%
  corrr::correlate(method = "pearson", use = "everything") %>%
  corrr::rplot() +
  theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, vjust = 0.7))

Yes, lots of high correlations. Interestingly, there are three variables in particular (uniformijjty, clean_cup and sweetness) which correlate moderately with eachother, and weakly with the others.
